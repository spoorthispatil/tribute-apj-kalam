Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

About Him

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, widely known as the "Missile Man of India", was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. A brilliant scientist and visionary, he played a crucial role in India's defense and space technology development.

He later served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and earned deep respect for his humility, intellect, and dedication to youth and education.

The Impact He Had

Dr. Kalam's life is a true example of how hard work, discipline, and dreams can lift a person from humble beginnings to the highest offices. Known for his love for teaching and connecting with students, he inspired millions of young minds across India to believe in their potential.

His legacy lives on through his books, speeches, and the continued work of scientists and dreamers he inspired.
